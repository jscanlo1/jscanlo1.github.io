About Me

My name is Johnny Scanlon. I have recently graduated from Trinity College Dublin with a master’s degree in computer engineering, in which i was awarded distinction (first class honours). I was a recipient of the prestigious Trinity foundation scholarship, for my academic performance in exams. My master’s thesis focused on Deep Learning and Natural Language Processing, as these are areas I'm particularly interested in.

Beyond just deep learning, I'm passionate about a plethora of scientific domains and how copmuyters can be applied to them, from speecha and audio processing to blockchains. Since graduating I've worked with Carbon27, a small tech startup, Carbon27, which looks at applying blockchain technology to the issue of carbon credits. Given the nature of small start-ups, this has allowed me to engage with issues from all areas of computer science, from low-level C coding on PCB boards, to blockchain smart contracts, and even some front-end UX problems.

Outside of Computer Engineering, I'm particularly passionate about reading and writing and pen my own short stories in my free time. On top of this I enjoy nature and hiking, and I also love to draw (when I have the time!).
